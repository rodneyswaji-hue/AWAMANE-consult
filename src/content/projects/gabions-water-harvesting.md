Seasonal rivers in Kenya's semi-arid counties carry enormous volumes of water during the rains — and stand dry for most of the year. Well-designed gabion structures can change that equation: slowing flow, recharging groundwater, building up sand deposits that act as natural water filters and reservoirs, and stabilizing riverbeds against erosion.

We have worked with communities to design, construct, and maintain these structures. The resulting sand is itself a valuable commodity, creating jobs for local youth when it is sustainably harvested after the rains.
