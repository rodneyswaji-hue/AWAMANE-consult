Lasting change in rural water management requires local skill, not outside experts flown in for one-off projects. Our training programs focus on building exactly that — equipping young people and women's groups with the practical skills to design, construct, maintain, and extend water harvesting infrastructure in their own communities.

Trainees graduate with hands-on experience across gabion construction, masonry basics, soil and water conservation structures, and the community organizing skills needed to see projects through to completion.
