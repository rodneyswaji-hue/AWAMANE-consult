Prof. Titus Ikusya Kanui is one of Kenya's most experienced animal scientists, with more than four decades of academic, research, and field experience spanning livestock production, animal physiology, and community-level agricultural development.

He currently serves as Professor of Animal Science at the School of Agriculture and Veterinary Sciences, South Eastern Kenya University (SEKU), a role he has held since 2010. Before joining SEKU, he spent over thirty years at the University of Nairobi — rising from Tutorial Fellow to Associate Professor in the Department of Veterinary Anatomy and Physiology.

His research contributions span animal nutrition, dairy science, poultry production, extension support services, and the scientific validation of plants used in traditional medicine for both livestock and human health. He has authored more than sixty peer-reviewed publications and supervised generations of agricultural scientists across East Africa.

At Awamane Consult Ltd, Prof. Kanui brings this academic depth directly to farms — translating decades of research and teaching into practical, affordable advice that works for smallholders, commercial farms, NGOs, and government agencies alike.
