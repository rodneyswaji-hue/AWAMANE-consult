Water is the single biggest constraint on agricultural productivity across Kenya's arid and semi-arid lands. Our water and land management work addresses this directly — helping farmers, communities, and organizations build the infrastructure and skills needed to capture, store, and use water efficiently.

Our field projects have included constructing gabions across seasonal streams for sand and water harvesting, setting up drip-irrigated vegetable demonstration plots, and training youth and women in the construction and maintenance of water harvesting structures. In many cases, these projects also create direct employment — for example, through organized sand harvesting after the rains.

We work at the intersection of environmental restoration, community livelihoods, and agricultural productivity — ensuring that water and land interventions deliver lasting value, not just short-term results.
